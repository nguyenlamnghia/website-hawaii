Who We Are

About Wellbeing Navigators Hawaii

A community-born nonprofit dedicated to making wellbeing accessible to every resident across the Hawaiian Islands — not just those who can afford it.

📍 Kaka'ako Community Center, Honolulu

Founded in 2019 by a small group of social workers and community advocates in Kaka'ako, Wellbeing Navigators Hawaii grew out of a simple frustration: too many of our neighbors were falling through the cracks. Mental health resources existed, but most people didn't know how to access them — or couldn't navigate the system alone.

We trained our first cohort of community navigators in early 2020, just weeks before the pandemic began. What started as a neighborhood outreach effort quickly became one of Honolulu's most requested community services. By 2022, we had expanded to Maui and the Big Island.

Today, our navigators walk alongside individuals facing mental health challenges, family hardship, grief, housing stress, and more — providing one-on-one support completely free of charge.

Our Process

How Our Navigation Works

From your first call to sustained community connection — here's what working with Wellbeing Navigators Hawaii looks like.

1

Reach Out — No Referral Needed

Call, email, or fill out our online form. No referral, no insurance, no paperwork. Anyone in Hawaii can connect with us directly.

2

Meet Your Navigator

Within 3 business days, you'll be matched with a trained navigator who understands your island community and cultural background.

3

Build Your Wellbeing Map

Together you'll identify what support you need — mental health, family resources, financial wellness, or something else — and create a clear action plan.

4

Navigate Together Until You're Ready

Your navigator walks with you through appointments, paperwork, and setbacks — as long as you need. There's no time limit.
